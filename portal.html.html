Module 1

The Philosophy Shift:
We Don't Sell Treatments.
We Guide Outcomes.

⏱ ~12 min read + workbook

The Most Important Shift in This Entire Course

Most medspas operate as treatment menus. The client picks. The provider executes. The client leaves having made a transactional decision based on what felt affordable that day.

That is not a clinical practice. That is a service counter. And it's why most medspas have average tickets under $500 despite having access to the same equipment, products, and techniques as the clinics doing $5K, $8K, even $12K per consultation.

The Dentist Comparison

Think about your last dental visit. Your dentist didn't ask you which procedure you wanted. They examined you, told you what they found, and gave you a clear clinical recommendation. You either accepted it or you didn't — but the recommendation came from their expertise, not from your guess.

That's exactly how a skin plan consultation should work. You assess. You diagnose. You recommend the path that produces the outcome the client actually wants. The client's job is to trust you and show up.

The Gym Analogy

Nobody walks into a gym and asks for a six-pack by Friday. Everyone understands intuitively that physical change requires a plan, consistency, and time. Skin works exactly the same way.

One laser treatment stimulates collagen. One peel can brighten pigment. But real, lasting, visible change — the kind that makes someone look genuinely younger — only happens when treatments are layered intentionally over time, with the right home care, consistently.

What Actually Changes When You Make This Shift

Average tickets go up because you're presenting full plans, not single treatments. Retention skyrockets because clients have a roadmap they're already committed to. Provider income grows because skin plans pay better than $90 dermaplane appointments. And your team stops feeling like they're "selling" — because they're not. They're recommending.

The Team System

⏱ ~12 min read + action step

The Silo Problem

In most medspas, providers operate in lanes. The aesthetician does facials. The nurse injector does injectables. They refer clients to each other occasionally, but it's informal and inconsistent. The result: every provider is leaving money on the table because they're only seeing their portion of what the client could be doing.

The skin plan model eliminates this completely. When every provider builds plans that draw from your full service menu, every provider starts generating business for every other provider — automatically, because the plan requires it.

How the Referral Loop Works

A nurse provider builds a skin plan for an anti-aging client. Her primary services — Morpheus8, injectables, EzGel — are naturally in the plan. But a comprehensive plan also calls for clinical facials, microneedling, and a professional skincare routine. So she builds all of it in — not as a referral, but as a required component of the outcome she's promising.

Now that client sees both the nurse and the aesthetician on a regular schedule. The aesthetician's book fills automatically. When the aesthetician identifies pigmentation or texture concerns during her treatments, she sends those clients back to the nurse for IPL or Morpheus8. The loop closes itself.

What This Looks Like in Practice

When your nurse injector builds a skin plan that includes Hydrafacials, your aesthetician's book fills automatically. When your aesthetician recommends Morpheus8, your nurse's book fills. The plan creates the referral — no manual handoffs, no awkward "you should also see..." conversations.

Every provider generates business for every other provider without any extra marketing dollar spent. This is the single most powerful operational shift in this entire course.

What to Tell Your Team

Frame this in your team meeting"Starting now, we're not assigning clients to providers. We're building plans that include all of us. Every plan you write should pull from the full menu. The nurse's plans include the aesthetician's services. The aesthetician's plans include the nurse's services. We're not competing — we're compounding."

Building Your Skin Plans

⏱ ~18 min read + workbook

The Three Rules of Building a Plan That Produces Real Results

Every skin plan that actually works follows three non-negotiable rules. Get all three right and your clients see results that make them tell every person they know. Get one wrong and the plan underperforms — and the client thinks the treatments didn't work.

Rule 1: Sequence

Certain treatments have to come before others. Morpheus8 always before EzGel — you want the collagen environment stimulated before introducing the biostimulator. Peels always before IPL — peeling the surface first allows the IPL to target deeper pigment more effectively. Get the order wrong and you're working against yourself.

Rule 2: Spacing

Hydrafacials always 2 weeks after active treatments — never sooner, never the same week. Morpheus8 sessions 4-6 weeks apart for optimal collagen response. Sculptra 4-6 weeks between sessions to allow the biostimulator to do its work. Timing between treatments drives results as much as the treatments themselves.

Rule 3: Combination

Which treatments enhance each other matters as much as which treatments you choose. Morpheus8 + EzGel is a synergistic pairing — the radiofrequency stimulates collagen, the biostimulator extends the results. IPL + chemical peels work together for layered pigmentation correction. Build plans around these proven combinations.

The 5 Categories — Every Plan Should Pull From at Least 4

Category 1 — Collagen Stimulation: Morpheus8, microneedling, RF treatments

Category 2 — Skin Hydration: Hydrafacials (always 2 weeks after active treatments)

Category 3 — Biostimulator: Sculptra, EzGel

Category 4 — Pigmentation/Redness Correction: Lumecca IPL

Category 5 — Chemical Peel: VI Peel series

The 4 Concern Categories

Anti-aging: Lead with Morpheus8 + EzGel + neurotoxin. Layer Hydrafacials, peels, biostimulators over 6-12 months.

Pigmentation: Lead with chemical peels first, then Lumecca IPL series. Support with brightening home care.

Texture: Microneedling + chemical peels + targeted home care.

Acne + scarring: Active acne treatment first (peels, extractions), then scar revision (Morpheus8, microneedling) once active acne is controlled.

The Consultation Script

⏱ ~20 min read + workbook

Know This Script Well Enough That It Sounds Like You

The goal is not to memorize this word for word. The goal is to internalize the structure and the key phrases, then deliver it in your own voice. The script only works when it sounds natural — not rehearsed.

Step 1 — The Skin Analysis

Before you say a single word about treatments, analyze the skin thoroughly. Take photos. Make observations out loud. When you take time to genuinely assess before saying anything, clients feel something most have never felt at a medspa — that you actually see them.

Step 2 — The Transition

The opening — memorize this"Based on everything we've talked about and what I'm seeing with your skin, I want to share what I think is the most effective path forward for you. My goal isn't just to do a treatment today — my goal is to actually get your skin where you want it to be. And to do that properly, we need a plan."

[Pause. Let it land. Then:]

"If your goal is real change — not just temporary improvement — this is the most effective way to get there."

Step 3 — Presenting the Plan

Walk through the plan month by month. Explain WHY each treatment is in there. Check in: "Does that make sense so far? Any questions about why I chose those?"

Step 4 — The Monthly Cost Reframe (Before Stating Total Price)

The monthly reframe"When you break this plan down monthly, you're looking at [$X/month]. That's less than most people spend on products sitting on their bathroom counter that aren't really doing much. But this is a complete guided program with a specific outcome at the end."

Step 5 — Stating the Price

State the price directly. Do not hedge. Do not apologize. Then stop talking. The silence after the price is the most powerful part of the entire consultation.

The Four Objections — Complete Responses

"It's too expensive.""I hear you — it's an investment. When you break it down monthly, it's [$X/month]. Most clients spend more than that on products that aren't doing a fraction of what this plan will do. The difference is everything in this plan is clinically proven to produce the specific results we talked about — and I'm guiding you through every step."
"I'm not sure I'll stick with it.""That's actually exactly why the plan works. You don't have to motivate yourself every month to decide whether to come in — the decision is already made. Clients who have a plan are so much more consistent than clients booking one treatment at a time."
"I've tried things before and they didn't work.""I hear this a lot and I genuinely understand the frustration. It's almost never that the treatments didn't work. It's that they weren't sequenced properly, weren't supported with the right home care, or there was no strategy connecting them. What we're building here is a system."
"Can I just do one treatment and see how it goes?""Absolutely — I want you to feel comfortable. One treatment will give you a result. What it won't do is get you to [their specific stated goal]. That takes a plan. So let's do this — let's start with [first treatment] today, and I'll send you the complete plan so you can see the whole picture. No pressure."

Pricing Your Plans

⏱ ~15 min read + workbook

Three Pricing Structures That Actually Work

Skin plans can be priced three different ways. Each works for different client types. Most clinics should offer all three.

Structure 1 — Pay In Full

The cleanest option. Client pays the full plan upfront, locks in the pricing, and is committed for the full duration. Offer a 5-10% discount for paying in full to incentivize this option. Best for: clients who want simplicity, can afford it, and want the savings.

Structure 2 — Monthly Payments

Through Affirm, CareCredit, Cherry, or your in-house financing. Break the total plan price across the duration. A $5,600 12-month plan becomes $467/month. Best for: most clients. The monthly framing is what makes premium plans feel attainable.

Structure 3 — Phase-Based

Break the plan into 3-month phases. Client pays Phase 1 upfront, then commits to Phase 2 at the end of Phase 1, and so on. Best for: clients who are nervous about a long commitment but want the structure of a plan.

The Three Tiers — How to Price Your Plans

Tier 1 (Foundation): $2,500 - $3,500. Basic plan addressing primary concern. 4-6 month duration. Best entry point for clients with smaller budgets.

Tier 2 (Comprehensive): $4,500 - $6,500. Multi-category plan addressing primary concern + supporting treatments. 6-9 month duration. This is your "sweet spot" — most clients land here.

Tier 3 (Premium): $8,000 - $12,000+. Full multi-modality plan with advanced treatments and biostimulators. 12-month duration. For clients ready to invest seriously.

Match the Plan to the Client

Don't default to the middle tier because it feels safer. If a client's stated goals require a Tier 3 plan, recommend it. Under-recommending is just as harmful as over-recommending — both fail to give the client what they actually need.

Home Care Integration

⏱ ~12 min read + workbook

Home Care Drives 80% of Long-Term Results

Every in-clinic treatment is supported by what happens in between visits. Without proper home care, even the best skin plan underperforms. Clients who follow a structured home care routine see dramatically better results than clients who don't.

The Standard Home Care Foundation — Every Client

Morning routine: Gentle cleanser → Antioxidant serum (Vitamin C) → Moisturizer → SPF 50+ (every single day, regardless of weather)

Evening routine: Cleanser → Active treatment (retinol, AHA, or BHA depending on concern) → Moisturizer → Targeted treatment

Home Care by Concern Category

Anti-aging: Add growth factor serum, peptide moisturizer, retinoid 3-4x per week, eye cream targeting fine lines.

Pigmentation: Add brightening serums (tranexamic acid, kojic acid, niacinamide), gentler exfoliation, strict daily SPF.

Texture: Add chemical exfoliants (glycolic, salicylic), retinol, hydrating serums, smoothing moisturizers.

Acne: Salicylic acid cleanser, benzoyl peroxide spot treatment, oil-free moisturizer, non-comedogenic SPF.

Why We Use Vivant Skincare

We've built our home care protocols around Vivant Skincare specifically because their formulations are clinical-grade, results-driven, and pair perfectly with the in-clinic treatments in our skin plans. Every plan we sell includes a Vivant home care prescription tailored to the client's concerns.

How to Sell Home Care

Frame home care as part of the plan"Your skincare routine at home is doing 80% of the work between visits. The treatments we do in clinic are the catalyst — the home care is what makes the results stick. I'm going to put together a routine specifically for your concerns. This isn't optional — it's part of the plan."

The Follow-Up System

⏱ ~14 min read + action step

Not Every Client Commits in the Room — That's Normal

Not getting an immediate yes is not a failure. It's a completely normal part of presenting skin plans. What matters is what you do after they leave. This module gives you a complete follow-up system regardless of what tools or technology you have available.

Send the Plan Within 24 Hours

Within 24 hours of any consultation where the client doesn't commit on the spot, send them their personalized plan. Include an expiration — 5 to 7 days. The expiration creates a natural deadline without any pressure coming from you personally.

5 Plan Delivery Options

You don't need a specific platform or software to deliver a skin plan. Choose the method that works best for your clinic right now.

Option 1 — Printed Plan (no tech required): Print the Skin Plan Client Template. Fill it out for the client. Hand it to them or mail it. Write the expiration date at the top. There is nothing wrong with a beautifully printed plan on quality paper — it can feel more premium than a digital version.

Option 2 — PDF via Email or Text: Fill out the template digitally, save as PDF, send directly. Free, fast, professional.

Option 3 — Google Doc or Canva Link: Create the plan in Google Docs or design it in Canva. Share via link. Canva plans can look extremely polished and on-brand.

Option 4 — RepeatMD: If your clinic uses RepeatMD, use its built-in plan-sending feature with automatic expiration dates and payment processing.

Option 5 — Your Booking Software: Square, Vagaro, Mindbody, Jane — most have package or treatment bundle features with payment links built in. Use what you already have.

The Follow-Up Message

When the plan expires (or 5-7 days have passed)"Hey [Name] — I noticed the plan I put together for you has expired. I wanted to reach out and see if there was anything about it you weren't sure about, or anything I could clarify for you. I want to make sure you have everything you need to make the decision that feels right for you."

The Second Follow-Up

7 days after the first follow-up if no response"Hey [Name] — just one more note and then I'll let you be. I'm still thinking about your skin and what we talked about. If the timing isn't right right now, that's completely okay — I'd love to reconnect when it is. Any questions, I'm always here."

After two follow-ups, stop. Two is appropriate. More becomes pressure and damages the relationship.

Maintenance: The Plan Never Truly Ends

⏱ ~14 min read

Plant the Seed From Day One

We never stop aging. Not after month six. Not after month twelve. Not ever. The critical mistake most providers make is waiting until the end of the plan to introduce the concept of maintenance. By that point it can feel like a surprise. Plant this seed at the very first consultation instead.

Say this at the first consultation — every time"One thing I want you to understand from the beginning is that we never stop aging. The plan we're building is going to get your skin to a significantly better place than it is today. And the maintenance plan we'll put together at the end is what keeps it there. Think of it like going to the gym — you don't just go for a year and then stop. You keep going because the results need to be maintained."

Why Maintenance Clients Are Your Best Clients

They already trust you completely. The hardest part of the client relationship is done. They've seen what consistency produces.

They require almost no convincing. A client who has watched their skin transform over 12 months doesn't need to be sold on maintenance.

They refer. Clients who complete a full skin plan have specific, visible, dramatic before-and-after results to show their friends. Those friends come in pre-sold.

The Maintenance Consultation

At the final appointment of every plan, pull up the before photos from their initial consultation side by side with current photos. Walk through the progress explicitly.

Now here's what I want to build with you — your maintenance protocol. The treatments you need going forward are far less intensive than what we've been doing. We've done the major work. Now we're maintaining."

What a Maintenance Plan Looks Like

Maintenance plans are lighter and less frequent — which means lower investment for the client and easier commitment.

Quarterly Morpheus8 or RF microneedling. Quarterly neurotoxin. Monthly Hydrafacials. Bi-annual biostimulator. Continued home care routine. Price as an annual commitment.

The Sentence That Closes Maintenance Every Time

The most powerful sentence in retention"Look at where we started versus where you are now. This is [X months] in. Imagine where we're going to be at month [X]."

That sentence — paired with the actual before-and-after photos — has retained more clients than any discount, promotion, or loyalty program we have ever tried at Glow.

Keeping Clients On Plan

⏱ ~12 min read + workbook

The 3-Touchpoint Retention System

Selling the plan is only half the battle. Keeping clients on plan over 6-12 months requires a structured retention system. We use a 3-touchpoint approach for every client between every appointment.

Touchpoint 1 — Within 48 Hours of Treatment

A simple post-care check-in message. Personal, brief, genuine.

Post-treatment check-in"Hey [Name] — just checking in to see how your skin is feeling after [treatment]. Any redness or sensitivity is completely normal at this stage. Let me know if you have any questions! Looking forward to seeing you for [next treatment]."

Touchpoint 2 — Halfway Between Appointments

A progress check-in. This is where most clinics drop off entirely. Don't.

Mid-cycle progress check"Hey [Name] — we're about halfway between treatments and I wanted to check in. How is the home care routine feeling? Are you noticing any changes? I'm always happy to adjust anything if needed."

Touchpoint 3 — Day Before Next Appointment

Confirmation + excitement-builder. Frame what's coming next.

Pre-appointment build-up"Hey [Name] — looking forward to seeing you tomorrow! We're doing [treatment] which is going to [specific benefit related to their plan]. This is a key piece of the timeline we built. See you at [time]."

The Pause Conversation

Some clients will want to pause. The pause conversation is rarely about pausing — it's about a real concern. Get curious instead of accommodating.

When a client says "I need to pause""Hey [Name] — I heard you wanted to pause and I just wanted to chat with you for a minute before we make any changes. I completely understand life happens. Before we pause anything, I want to ask — what does pause look like to you? And is the pause about the financial side, or is it that something about the plan isn't working for you?"

Often the answer is fixable without actually pausing. Push appointments out, restructure payments, address the real concern. Pausing the plan breaks momentum and most plans never resume.

The provider called the client back personally:

"Hey [Name] — I heard you wanted to pause and I just wanted to chat with you for a minute. Before we pause anything, I want to ask — what does pause look like to you? Are we talking a few weeks or longer-term? And is the pause about finances, or is something about the plan not working?"

Client revealed: An unexpected medical bill. She was overwhelmed. Wanted to pause but felt bad because she was happy with the results.

Provider's solution:

"Here's what I want to do. We're not going to pause — because if we pause, the spacing between treatments will throw off the entire sequence and you'll lose momentum. Instead, I'm going to push your next two treatments out by 6 weeks each, which gives you breathing room without breaking the plan. And we can adjust your payment schedule too. Does that work?"

Result: Client stayed on plan. Did not pause. Completed the full 12-month plan. Rolled into maintenance.

The lesson: The pause conversation is rarely about pausing. It's about a real concern — financial, scheduling, or doubt about results. Get curious. Most pauses are solvable without actually pausing.

The Staff Incentive System

⏱ ~15 min read + workbook

The Final Piece: Staff Incentives That Actually Work

You can build the perfect skin plan system, write the most polished consultation script, design the most sophisticated treatment menu — and none of it will work consistently across your team unless your providers have a financial reason to use it.

Staff incentives don't motivate effort. They direct attention. When the financial outcome of selling skin plans is clear and immediate, providers learn the skill faster because they have a reason to.

The Tiered Bonus Structure

This is the exact bonus framework we use at Glow. The tiers correspond to the three plan price points from Module 5.

Tier 1 — Plans $2,500-$3,500: $50 bonus per plan sold

Tier 2 — Plans $4,500-$6,500: $150 bonus per plan sold

Tier 3 — Plans $8,000 and above: $300 bonus per plan sold

The numbers are intentional. The jump from $50 to $150 to $300 is significant enough that providers feel the difference between presenting a Tier 1 versus a Tier 3 plan. They start naturally building bigger plans because the financial outcome of doing so is meaningful.

The Cross-Provider Acknowledgment Bonus

When a provider builds a skin plan that includes another provider's services and that plan is purchased, the originating provider receives an additional $25 bonus on top of the tier bonus. This rewards them for thinking about the full client journey instead of just their own services.

Why Bonuses Beat Commission

Commission creates competition between providers. Bonuses create collaboration. When every provider gets a bonus regardless of whose client they served, the entire team works together to grow plans. When providers compete for commission, they hoard, they don't refer, and the team system collapses.

How to Roll This Out to Your Team

Week 1: Hold a team meeting. Walk through the bonus structure and the math. Show specific examples of how a provider's monthly income could change.

Week 2: Provide each provider with the consultation script and plan templates. Practice presentations as a team.

Week 3: Soft launch — providers start using the new system but you don't track bonuses yet.

Week 4 onwards: Bonuses go live. First payout at the end of the month creates instant momentum.

Tracking Bonuses

You don't need fancy software. A simple spreadsheet works. Each row is a plan sold. Columns: provider name, plan amount, tier, bonus earned, cross-provider bonus, date paid. Pay bonuses monthly. Don't make providers wait quarterly.
