The Team System

⏱ ~12 min read + action step

The Silo Problem

In most medspas, providers operate in lanes. The aesthetician does facials. The nurse injector does injectables. They refer clients to each other occasionally, but it’s informal and inconsistent. The result: every provider is leaving money on the table because they’re only seeing their portion of what the client could be doing.

The skin plan model eliminates this completely. When every provider builds plans that draw from your full service menu, every provider starts generating business for every other provider — automatically, because the plan requires it.

How the Referral Loop Works

A nurse provider builds a skin plan for an anti-aging client. Her primary services — Morpheus8, injectables, EzGel — are naturally in the plan. But a comprehensive plan also calls for clinical facials, microneedling, and a professional skincare routine. So she builds all of it in — not as a referral, but as a required component of the outcome she’s promising.

Now that client sees both the nurse provider and the aesthetician. Both work toward the same goal. The nurse’s collagen stimulators amplify what the aesthetician is doing. The aesthetician’s facials support the nurse’s energy device results. Both providers grow their books. The client gets better outcomes.

Key mindset shift for your team"You are not recommending services. You are building a treatment sequence that gets this specific client to their specific goal. Every service in the plan is there because it belongs there — not because anyone is trying to sell something."

How to Train Your Team

Hold a team meeting. Lay out your full service menu. Walk through a sample client case. Ask every provider: what would you add to this plan from your menu? Do this with three or four client profiles — anti-aging, pigmentation, texture, acne. What you’ll find is that a comprehensive plan for almost any skin concern naturally touches at least two or three service categories.

The Three Rules of Treatment Layering

These apply to every plan you build regardless of client, concern, or budget. All three factors must be correct for a plan to perform at its highest level.

Rule 1
Sequence

Certain treatments must come before others. Front-load heavy collagen stimulation so results compound throughout the plan.

Rule 2
Spacing

Timing between treatments drives results. Hydrafacials always 2 weeks after active treatments. Morpheus8 sessions 4–6 weeks apart.

Rule 3
Combination

Which treatments enhance each other. Morpheus8 + EzGel = synergistic collagen stimulation. Peels before IPL — never after.

The Four Concern Categories — Sequencing Rules

Anti-Aging / Aging SkinLines · Laxity · Volume Loss

Sequence: Morpheus8 first — it creates the collagen foundation everything else builds on. Collagen results continue developing for 3–6 months after treatment, so the client keeps improving between appointments. Neurotoxin every 3 months throughout. EzGel at points where the collagen environment is already stimulated and most responsive. Hydrafacials 2 weeks after every active treatment.

Sample 6-month sequence: Month 1: Morpheus8 + facial → Month 2: EzGel + neurotoxin + facial → Month 3: Morpheus8 + microneedling → Month 4: Hydrafacial + peel → Month 5: Morpheus8 + EzGel → Month 6: Photo review + neurotoxin + maintenance plan.

Pigmentation / Sun Damage / MelasmaSunspots · Redness · Uneven Tone

Critical rule: Peels before Lumecca IPL — always. Peeling the surface first allows IPL to work on deeper pigment more effectively and reduces the risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. For melasma: conservative approach first. Avoid heat-based treatments until melasma is well-managed because heat is a known trigger.

Sample 6-month sequence: Month 1: VI Peel + brightening facial → Month 2: VI Peel + facial → Month 3: Lumecca IPL + Hydrafacial → Month 4: Lumecca IPL + peel → Month 5: Lumecca IPL + facial → Month 6: Photo review + spot treatment + maintenance.

Non-negotiable: SPF 50 every single morning is the make-or-break of any pigmentation plan. Say this out loud at every appointment.

Texture / Pores / Overall Skin QualitySmoothness · Refinement · Glow

Why this category is your best first-plan client: Results are often the fastest and most visible of all four categories. Significant improvement is visible within 60–90 days. These clients make great first plans because early visible results build trust in the method quickly.

Sample 6-month sequence: Month 1: SkinPen microneedling + Hydrafacial → Month 2: VI Peel + Hydrafacial → Month 3: SkinPen + Lumecca IPL (if redness present) → Month 4: Hydrafacial + peel → Month 5: SkinPen final session → Month 6: Photo review + Morpheus8 if laxity present + maintenance.

Acne / Breakouts / ScarringTwo-Phase Protocol

Non-negotiable rule: Phase 1 = clear the active acne. Phase 2 = treat the scarring. Never simultaneously treat active acne and scarring aggressively. Doing so inflames the skin, worsens breakouts, and causes additional post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. Be explicit with the client about this from the first consultation.

Phase 1 (Months 1–4): Clinical acne facials + VI Peels + Lumecca IPL (reduces bacteria and redness) → assess at Month 4. If clear, begin Phase 2.

Phase 2 (Months 5–12): SkinPen microneedling series → Morpheus8 for rolling/boxcar scars → Hydrafacials throughout → Lumecca IPL for residual pigmentation. Photo review at Month 6 and 12.

Know This Script Well Enough That It Sounds Like You

The goal is not to memorize this word for word. The goal is to internalize the structure and the key phrases, then deliver it in your own voice. The script only works when it sounds natural — not rehearsed.

Step 1 — The Skin Analysis

Before you say a single word about treatments, analyze the skin thoroughly. Take photos. Make observations out loud. When you take time to genuinely assess before saying anything, clients feel something most have never felt at a medspa — that you actually see them.

"I'm noticing some laxity along the jawline here." "This is classic sun damage accumulating in the mid-forties." "Your pores are enlarged through the center panel — that tells me something about your oil production." Specific, accurate observations build immediate trust.

Step 2 — The Transition

The opening — memorize this"Based on everything we've talked about and what I'm seeing with your skin, I want to share what I think is the most effective path forward for you. My goal isn't just to do a treatment today — my goal is to actually get your skin where you want it to be. And to do that properly, we need a plan."

[Pause. Let it land. Then:]

"If your goal is real change — not just temporary improvement — this is the most effective way to get there."

[Pause again. This pause is not dead air. It is intentional space. Let the gravity of what you said land.]

Walking through the plan"So here's what I'm recommending for you over the next [6/12] months. In the first phase — months one through [X] — we're going to focus on [primary concern]. We'll be doing [treatment 1] because [specific reason], and [treatment 2] because [specific reason]."

[Check in: "Does that make sense so far? Any questions about why I chose those?"]

"The reason I build plans this way is the same reason a personal trainer builds a full workout program — every single thing we do is designed to build on what came before it. By month three you'll start noticing real changes. By month six, the people around you are going to notice."

Pricing Your Plans

⏱ ~14 min read + worksheet

Price the Outcome, Not the Appointments

A client on a 6-month skin plan is not paying for a series of appointments. They are paying to look visibly clearer, firmer, and healthier at the end of the plan. They are paying for the expertise behind the plan, the accountability built into the structure, and the certainty that comes from having someone who knows exactly where they’re taking them. Price from that value.

The Three Pricing Structures

Pay in Full + Discount

Calculate full retail of every treatment. Offer 10–15% for full commitment. Rewards the client, rewards your cash flow.

"If you booked each separately you’d be at [$X]. Because you’re committing to the full plan, we do it for [$Y]."

Monthly Framing

Break the total into a monthly figure. A $5,600 plan = $933/month. That’s a gym membership framing and it lands very differently.

"When you break this down monthly you’re looking at [$X/month] — less than most people spend on products that aren’t doing much."

Phase-Based Payment

Price the plan in two phases. Lower entry investment. After Phase 1 results, Phase 2 is rarely a difficult conversation.

"Let’s start with Phase 1 at [$X]. Once you see where your skin is at that point, Phase 2 almost sells itself."

Module 7

The Follow-Up System

⏱ ~14 min read + action step

Not Every Client Commits in the Room — That's Normal

Not getting an immediate yes is not a failure. It's a completely normal part of presenting skin plans. What matters is what you do after they leave. This module gives you a complete follow-up system regardless of what tools or technology you have available.

Step 1 — Send the Plan After the Consultation

Within 24 hours of any consultation where the client doesn't commit on the spot, send them their personalized plan. The key is including an expiration — 5 to 7 days. The expiration creates a natural deadline without any pressure coming from you personally. It also gives you a legitimate reason to follow up.

HOW TO DELIVER THE PLAN — 5 OPTIONS

You do not need a specific platform or software to deliver a skin plan. Choose the method that works best for your clinic right now and upgrade later if needed.

Option 1 — Printed Plan (no tech required)

Print the Skin Plan Client Template from your downloads folder. Fill it out for the client during or after the consultation. Hand it to them before they leave or mail it to them. Write the expiration date at the top: "This plan is reserved for you until [date]." This is the simplest, most personal option and works beautifully for clinics that prefer a hands-on approach. There is nothing wrong with a beautifully printed plan on quality paper — it can feel more premium than a digital version.

Option 2 — PDF via Email or Text

Fill out the Skin Plan Client Template digitally, save it as a PDF, and email or text it directly to the client with a note like: "I put your personalized plan together — take a look and let me know if you have any questions. I've held this pricing for you until [date]." Free, fast, and completely professional.

Option 3 — Google Doc or Canva Link

Create a simple personalized plan in Google Docs or design it in Canva. Share the link via text or email. Canva plans can look extremely polished and on-brand with your clinic's colors and logo. Set a reminder to follow up when the link expires.

Option 4 — RepeatMD

If your clinic uses RepeatMD, it has a built-in plan-sending feature with automatic expiration dates and payment processing. Set a 5–7 day expiration. The client receives a digital plan they can purchase directly. When it expires, you get a notification which is your trigger to send the follow-up message.

Option 5 — Your Booking Software or POS

Many booking platforms (Square, Vagaro, Mindbody, Jane) allow you to create packages or treatment bundles and send them to clients with a payment link. Build the plan as a package in your existing software, send the payment link, and set a calendar reminder to follow up in 5–7 days. No new software needed.

Step 2 — The Follow-Up Message

When the plan expires or 5–7 days have passed, reach out with exactly this energy: you're checking in because you care about their outcome, not because you want their money.

The follow-up message — send when plan expires"Hey [Name] — I noticed the plan I put together for you has expired. I wanted to reach out and see if there was anything about it you weren't sure about, or anything I could clarify for you. I want to make sure you have everything you need to make the decision that feels right for you."

Step 3 — The Second Follow-Up

If the first follow-up gets no response, send one more message seven days later. After this — stop. Two follow-ups is appropriate. More than two becomes pressure and damages the relationship.

Second and final follow-up"Hey [Name] — just one more note and then I'll let you be. I'm still thinking about your skin and what we talked about. If the timing isn't right right now, that's completely okay — I'd love to reconnect when it is. Any questions, I'm always here."

Module 8

Maintenance: The Plan Never Truly Ends

⏱ ~14 min read

Plant the Seed From Day One

We never stop aging. Not after month six. Not after month twelve. Not ever. This is not a frightening thing to tell a client — it's the natural, honest reason why maintenance after the plan is as important as the plan itself.

The critical mistake most providers make is waiting until the end of the plan to introduce the concept of maintenance. By that point it can feel like a surprise — like you're extending something they thought was over. Plant this seed at the very first consultation instead.

Say this at the first consultation — every time"One thing I want you to understand from the beginning is that we never stop aging. The plan we're building is going to get your skin to a significantly better place than it is today. And the maintenance plan we'll put together at the end is what keeps it there. Think of it like going to the gym — you don't just go for a year and then stop. You keep going because the results need to be maintained."

Why Maintenance Clients Are Your Best Clients

A client transitioning from a 6 or 12-month plan into maintenance is the most valuable client in your clinic. Here's why:

They already trust you completely. The hardest part of the client relationship — building trust, demonstrating results, getting commitment — is done. They've seen what consistency produces. They've experienced your plan working. They're not skeptical. They're believers.

They require almost no convincing. A client who has watched their skin transform over 12 months doesn't need to be sold on maintenance. They've seen what stopping looks like in the past. They know what consistency produces. The maintenance conversation is one of the easiest closes in the entire system.

They refer. Clients who complete a full skin plan and see real results are your most powerful source of referrals. They have specific, visible, dramatic before-and-after results to show their friends. Those friends come in pre-sold on the method.

The Maintenance Consultation

At the final appointment of every plan, pull up the before photos from their initial consultation side by side with current photos. Walk through the progress explicitly and specifically. Never assume they can see what you see — tell them.

The maintenance close"Look at what we've done in [X months]. Look at [specific improvement]. Look at what's changed in [specific area]. This is exactly on track. Now here's what I want to build with you — your maintenance protocol. The treatments you need going forward are far less intensive than what we've been doing. We've done the major work. Now we're maintaining the results."

What a Maintenance Plan Looks Like

Maintenance plans are lighter and less frequent than active plans — which means lower investment for the client and easier commitment. A typical maintenance plan might include:

Quarterly Morpheus8 or RF microneedling to maintain collagen levels. Quarterly neurotoxin on the same schedule. Monthly clinical facials or Hydrafacials to keep the skin healthy and receptive. Bi-annual biostimulator treatment to continue slow collagen building. Continued home care routine — this never stops.

Price the maintenance plan as an annual commitment. Many clients will roll directly from their initial plan into maintenance without any gap. That is the best possible outcome — it means they've experienced the results, they trust you, and they're committed to the long game.

What to Do When Clients Want to Stop

Some clients will reach the end of their plan and hesitate about committing to maintenance. Here is how to handle this without pressure.

When a client is hesitant about maintenance"I completely understand — you've made a significant investment and I get that you might want to take a breath. Here's what I want you to know about what happens if we pause. The results we've built together won't disappear overnight. But they will gradually diminish, because aging continues regardless of what we do or don't do. The maintenance plan is designed to preserve exactly what we've achieved — and the good news is that maintaining results is much less intensive and much less expensive than building them."

Most clients who have genuinely experienced 12 months of plan results don't need much convincing. They've seen what consistency produces. They know what stopping looks like. The conversation takes care of itself when you approach it from a place of genuine care rather than a sales agenda.

The Sentence That Closes Maintenance Every Time

The most powerful sentence in retention"Look at where we started versus where you are now. This is [X months] in. Imagine where we're going to be at month [X]."

That sentence — paired with the actual before-and-after photos — has retained more clients than any discount, promotion, or loyalty program we have ever tried at Glow. The photos are proof. The plan is working. There is no rational reason to stop. Show them the evidence and let it speak for itself.

Keeping Clients On Plan

⏱ ~14 min read + action step

The Three-Touchpoint System

The biggest risk to the skin plan model is client dropout. Life gets busy. Results start to look good enough. The urgency of the next appointment fades. Your job is to stay connected in a way that keeps the plan feeling alive between visits.

Touchpoint 1 — Post-Appointment (within 24 hours)
Send this after every visit"So glad we got that session in today — your skin looked incredible. Next up is [treatment] in [X weeks], which is going to build beautifully on what we did today. You’re going to love where this takes us."

This message reminds them of the next appointment, creates anticipation, and reinforces that you’re actively thinking about their journey between visits.

Touchpoint 2 — 30-Day Check-In
Send 30 days after first appointment"Hey [Name] — just checking in on how your skin is feeling. How’s the home care routine going? Any questions or anything that’s been on your mind? I’m always here."

This catches dropout risk early. If a client has any concerns, this message opens the door before it becomes a reason to stop coming in.

Touchpoint 3 — Midpoint Photo Review

At the halfway point, pull up the before photos from their initial consultation side by side with current photos. Walk through the progress explicitly. This is your most powerful retention tool.

The most powerful retention sentence"Look at where we started versus where you are now. This is [X months] in. Imagine where we’re going to be at month [X]."

Clients who can see their own measurable progress do not stop coming in. The photos are proof. This sentence paired with real before-and-afters has retained more clients than any discount we have ever run.

The Final Piece: Staff Incentives That Actually Work

You can build the perfect skin plan system, write the most polished consultation script, design the most sophisticated treatment menu — and none of it will work consistently across your team unless your providers have a financial reason to use it.

Staff incentives don't motivate effort. They direct attention. When the financial outcome of selling skin plans is clear and immediate, providers learn the skill faster because they have a reason to.

The Tiered Bonus Structure

This is the exact bonus framework we use at Glow. The tiers correspond to the three plan price points from Module 5.

Tier 1 — Plans $2,500-$3,500: $50 bonus per plan sold

Tier 2 — Plans $4,500-$6,500: $150 bonus per plan sold

Tier 3 — Plans $8,000 and above: $300 bonus per plan sold

The numbers are intentional. The jump from $50 to $150 to $300 is significant enough that providers feel the difference between presenting a Tier 1 versus a Tier 3 plan. They start naturally building bigger plans because the financial outcome of doing so is meaningful.

The Cross-Provider Acknowledgment Bonus

This is the bonus that makes your team system actually function. Module 2 talks about the referral loop — when nurse providers build plans that include aesthetician services, both books fill. The cross-provider bonus rewards that behavior explicitly.

Cross-provider acknowledgmentWhen a provider builds a skin plan that includes another provider's services and that plan is purchased, the originating provider receives an additional $25 bonus on top of the tier bonus. This rewards them for thinking about the full client journey instead of just their own services.

Why Bonuses Beat Commission

Some clinic owners want to use straight commission instead of tiered bonuses. We tried this. It didn't work. Here's why.

Commission creates competition between providers. Bonuses create collaboration. When every provider gets a bonus regardless of whose client they served, the entire team starts working together to grow plans across the clinic. When providers compete for commission on individual clients, they hoard, they don't refer, and the team system collapses.

Tiered bonuses also have a clearer ceiling and floor for budgeting. You know exactly what each plan costs you in bonus payouts. With commission, your costs scale unpredictably with revenue.

How to Roll This Out to Your Team

Don't surprise your team with a new bonus structure. Announce it as a positive change with a clear timeline. Here is the rollout we recommend:

Week 1: Hold a team meeting. Walk through the bonus structure and the math. Show specific examples of how a provider's monthly income could change with the new system.

Week 2: Provide each provider with the consultation script and plan templates. Practice presentations as a team.

Week 3: Soft launch — providers start using the new system but you don't track bonuses yet. Everyone gets comfortable.

Week 4 onwards: Bonuses go live. First payout at the end of the month creates instant momentum.

Tracking Bonuses

You don't need fancy software for this. A simple spreadsheet works. Each row is a plan sold. Columns: provider name, plan amount, tier, bonus earned, cross-provider bonus earned, date paid.

Pay bonuses monthly. Don't make providers wait quarterly — the immediate feedback loop is what makes the system work.

The Long-Term Compounding Effect

Here is what happens after 6-12 months of running the bonus system consistently.

Your top providers become wealthier and more loyal. They have specific financial reasons to stay at your clinic. Recruiters can't easily poach them with promises of higher base salaries because the bonus structure provides upside that's hard to match.

Your newer providers grow faster than they would anywhere else. The bonus structure incentivizes them to learn the consultation script, build full plans, and develop their skills aggressively. Within 6-12 months they're performing at the level of providers with twice their experience elsewhere.

Your clinic culture shifts. Providers start helping each other build better plans because they all benefit when one of them succeeds. The cross-provider bonus reinforces this.
